### Concepts Locked In
- A switch is a device with an array of ports on it's interface. 
	It's used to congregate local devices which creates a LAN. A switch operates at Layer 2 and uses MAC addresses to forward frames to the right port.
- A router connects different networks and forwards packets between them using IP addresses.
- A client is a device which requests a service from a server. A server is a device which hosts services for a client 
- A firewall sits between two devices, or a device and a network ( a router and a switch vs a router and the internet).
	It acts based on rules of allowed traffic vs denied traffic. There are two different types of firewalls - 
	Host Based: A software installed on a device designed to protect that machine. Network-Based: A firewall placed at the edge of a network designed to protect the devices inside of it.
