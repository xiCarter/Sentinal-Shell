### Concepts Locked In
- ARP (Address Resolution Protocol) — used to
  dynamically learn MAC addresses on a network
- When a device doesn't know the MAC address of
  a destination it broadcasts an ARP request to
  every connected device
- The destination responds directly to the source
  with its MAC address — source records it
- Both source and destination learn each other's
  MAC addresses through this exchange
- ARP only fires when the destination MAC is
  unknown — once it's in the table, frames go
  directly without broadcasting
- Switches build MAC address tables dynamically
  by reading the source MAC of every incoming frame
- First ping is slower — ARP round trip happens
  before ICMP packets even go out
  Subsequent pings are faster — MAC already cached
