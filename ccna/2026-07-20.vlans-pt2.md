### Key Concepts
- Access port carries ONE VLAN; trunk port carries MANY VLANs over a single link
- 802.1Q inserts a VLAN tag into the frame header so the receiving switch knows which VLAN it belongs to
- The tag is added when a frame enters the trunk and stripped when it exits to an access port
- Native VLAN = the one VLAN sent UNTAGGED across the trunk (default VLAN 1)
- Native VLAN must match on both ends of a trunk or traffic mismatches
- Trunking is what collapses the three separate R1-to-SW1 links from Day 16 into ONE link
- This is the foundation for router-on-a-stick — one physical link, subinterfaces per VLAN
